# Standardized-Community-Vocational-Training-Unit-of-Toujane-
Vocational Training Center – Tozeur, Tunisia
Overview
The Vocational Training Center in Tozeur, Tunisia is an open architectural project focused on vocational education in textile production, local gastronomy and tourism.

The project is conceived as an open design system in which the way the building is designed, constructed and operated is understandable, accessible and reproducible by anyone. Rather than being a unique architectural object, the project is intended to function as a shared framework that can be adapted, modified and rebuilt in different locations according to local needs, available resources and climatic conditions.

All design information is publicly documented and distributed through architectural drawings, CAD files, 3D models and construction details. This documentation allows architects, students, researchers and local communities to study, reproduce and further develop the project.

Open Design Approach
The project follows the principles of open-source architecture.

All available documentation is shared through this repository, including:

Architectural drawings

CAD documentation

3D models

Construction details

Material studies

Environmental analysis

Design development files

By making the design process transparent and accessible, the project promotes knowledge sharing and encourages adaptation in different social and geographical contexts.

Educational and Community Participation
Construction is organized as a participatory learning process involving:

Local communities

Craftspeople

Builders

Students

Trainees

Through workshops and hands-on construction activities participants learn how the building is assembled and actively contribute to its realization.

The building therefore acts both as an educational facility and as a learning platform during its own construction.

Construction System
The project combines low-tech building methods with contemporary design tools.

Primary Materials
Reinforced Concrete

Rammed Earth

Glass

Thermal Insulation Materials

Timber Components

Natural Shading Elements

Material selection is based on durability, availability and climatic performance.

Rammed earth provides thermal mass and helps reduce indoor temperatures during hot periods.

North-facing glazed surfaces support natural ventilation and daylighting while minimizing excessive solar gain.

Environmental Strategy
The architectural proposal responds to the climatic conditions of Southern Tunisia through:

High thermal mass construction

Passive cooling strategies

Natural cross ventilation

Shaded outdoor spaces

Semi-permeable façade elements

Reduced solar heat gain

Locally sourced materials

These strategies contribute to lower energy consumption and improved thermal comfort.

Project Vision
The Vocational Training Center is not simply a building.

It is a framework for sharing knowledge, developing local skills and encouraging collective participation in the creation of the built environment.

The project seeks to demonstrate how architecture can become a collaborative, adaptable and open process through which communities gain the knowledge and capacity to design and construct future projects independently.
